# RFM & Behavioral Retention Strategy

## Overview
This document outlines our targeted retention strategy using RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) metrics augmented with behavioral signals (Support Ticket Count and Last Visit Days Ago). By moving away from indiscriminate discounting, we can optimize campaign budgets and maximize ROI.

## Segmentation Definitions & Actions

### 1. Champions
- **Definition:** Recency $\le$ 30 days, Frequency $\ge$ 3 orders, Monetary $>$ 1500 INR.
- **Customer Behavior:** Highly engaged, recent buyers who spend significantly.
- **Recommended Action:** **Loyalty & Advocacy.** Do not offer discounts. Instead, offer early access to new product launches, exclusive 'Platinum' loyalty tier upgrades, and referral bonuses.
- **Expected Value:** Very High. They drive word-of-mouth and predictable revenue.

### 2. High-Value but Unhappy Customers
- **Definition:** Monetary $\ge$ 1000 INR AND Support Tickets $\ge$ 2.
- **Customer Behavior:** These customers spend heavily but have experienced friction (multiple support tickets, returns, or delays).
- **Recommended Action:** **White-Glove Intervention.** Route these accounts to a dedicated customer success agent. Issue an apology paired with a high-value 'Bundle Discount' to rebuild trust.
- **Expected Value:** High. Rescuing these accounts prevents the loss of top-tier revenue.

### 3. Loyal Customers
- **Definition:** Recency $\le$ 60 days, Frequency $\ge$ 2 orders.
- **Customer Behavior:** Steady purchasers with reliable behavior.
- **Recommended Action:** **Upsell & Cross-sell.** Promote categories they haven't tried yet. No heavy discounts needed.
- **Expected Value:** High steady revenue.

### 4. At-Risk Customers
- **Definition:** Fall outside the other buckets, generally Recency 60-120 days or declining frequency.
- **Customer Behavior:** Slipping engagement. They are in the window where a nudge can reactivate them.
- **Recommended Action:** **Win-Back Offers.** Send a targeted 'Free Shipping' or moderate discount via email and SMS.

### 5. Low-Engagement Customers
- **Definition:** Frequency = 1, Last Visit $>$ 20 days ago.
- **Customer Behavior:** One-time buyers who have stopped interacting digitally.
- **Recommended Action:** **Re-onboarding.** Send educational content about the brand/category. If no response, use a 'Welcome Offer' style aggressive discount as a last resort.

### 6. Discount-Sensitive Customers
- **Definition:** Average Discount Used $>$ 30%.
- **Customer Behavior:** They only buy when sales are active.
- **Recommended Action:** **Margin Protection.** Exclude them from evergreen campaigns. Only target them during major liquidation or seasonal sales events to protect brand equity.

### 7. Dormant Customers
- **Definition:** Recency $>$ 120 days.
- **Customer Behavior:** Long gone. Reacquisition is often as expensive as acquiring a new customer.
- **Recommended Action:** **De-prioritize.** Stop active spending. Keep on low-cost generic email newsletters.

---

## Campaign Budget Prioritization
Assuming a constrained marketing budget, funds should be allocated in the following priority order:
1. **High-Value but Unhappy (Priority 1):** Highest ROI on retention spend. Losing a >1000 INR customer over a solvable support issue is a major loss.
2. **At-Risk Customers (Priority 2):** Best window of opportunity to intervene before they enter the "Dormant" stage.
3. **Low-Engagement Customers (Priority 3):** Allocate remaining budget to convert one-time buyers into repeat purchasers.

*Champions and Loyal Customers* require zero direct discounting budget. *Discount-Sensitive* and *Dormant* customers should be excluded from active paid retention.
